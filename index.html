Langley

Langley is a town on the Western side of the island. Not only does it have waterfront views of the Cascade Mountains, it also has lots of shops and restaurants to visit. Click here to learn more about my favorite places in Langley.

Keep your eyes open for bunnies around Langley. Hundreds of bunnies call Langley home and on hot days, they will find shade under parked cars. You can read more about it here.

Coupeville

Coupeville is on the Northwestern side of the island and is one of Whidbey's oldest towns. Visitors can walk to the end of the warf where the building is set up with a full whale skelaton and other facts about the native sea life.

In Coupeville? Consider swinging over to Ebey's Landing.

Ebey's Landing is a National Park near Coupeville. It has a stunning view of the Puget Sound and, on a clear day, you can see Mt. Rainier and Mt. Baker.

Deception Pass

Deception Pass is on the Northern end of the island and is the strait that separates Whidbey from Fidalgo Island. It includes a campground where you can camp and multiple beaches. One of the best views is from Deception Pass Bridge.
